The context

The operational blind spot

There is no clarity on how many garments circulate, where they are lost, their real service life, which service rotates the most, or the annual cost of attrition.

Operational risks

What is at stake without control

  • Handling of soiled linen.
  • Occupational exposure.
  • Difficulties during audits.
  • Disputes over counts.
  • Lack of traceability by service.
What changes

What changes with the group's solution

  • Individual identification.
  • Record of wash cycles.
  • Control by area / service.
  • Early loss detection.
  • Reports for management.
Manageable indicators

What becomes measurable

  • Attrition by service.
  • Rotation.
  • Cycles before retirement.
  • Real inventory.
  • Compliance by area.
Impact by role

What it solves at each level

Management

Textile cost control and compliance evidence to support decision-making.

Logistics / Linen room

Control by area and service, early loss detection and real available inventory.

Infection control and audit

Traceability by service and demonstrable data for internal and external audits.
